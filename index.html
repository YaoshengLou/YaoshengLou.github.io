About Me

Image generated by ChatGPT (DALL·E)

I am currently a PhD student in Educational Psychology and Methodology at the University at Albany, SUNY. My research focuses on methodological innovations and applications in quantitative synthesis (i.e., meta-analysis) of single-case experimental design (SCED) studies.

With a background in computer science (minor), I’m also deeply interested in how artificial intelligence can enhance educational research methods. From automating data extraction to supporting effect size decisions, I explore how AI tools can support more efficient and rigorous research workflows.

In my spare time, I enjoy finding creative ways to explain complex statistics without putting students to sleep, having serious conversations with ChatGPT, and daydreaming about a world where code always runs on the first try...
